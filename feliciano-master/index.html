Colonel's Story

Colonel's Timeline

Self-determined as the Colonel was, at age 16, he lied about his age in order to enlist in the army.

1906

The Colonel had quite a colourful resume.

1920

The Colonel cooked for hungry travellers at his service station where he added fried chicken to the menu.

1930

The Colonel perfected his Original Recipe chicken with his secret blend of 11 herbs and spices, pressure-cooked to seal in the finger lickin' goodness.

1939

At 65, after retirement, Colonel Sanders devoted himself to franchising his chicken business, travelling from restaurant to restaurant across the U.S. and Canada by car.

1950

A man who never retired, at age 75 it was the beginning for the Colonel. He sold his interest in the company for $2 million (that's about $14,161,464 today!) and KFC continued to grow globally.

1965

The Colonel, a figurehead in his own right, was ranked as the world's second most recognizable celebrity.

1976

Diagnosed with acute leukemia in June, he appeared before crowds in his signature white woolen suit and string tie and remained active until the month before his death. The Colonel died of pneumonia in Louisville, Kentucky, on December 16 at the age of 90.
